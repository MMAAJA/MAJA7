ANDRES VÉLEZ

EMPTY.FIRE.WATERPIEDRA.

ROCK

SCULPTURE

It is essential in art to create contexts that highlight the malleability of the environment. The sensitivity, the imagination, the look, the feeling of the artist are fundamental elements in this commitment. In the work of Andrés Vélez, the stones cease to be stones to become a declaration of balance, stream of water, fire lit or inasible lightness. The stones are at the origin of experiences and reflections around solidity, permanence and natural beauty. Andrés Vélez is a sculptor, stone carver, creator of perceptions, professor of sculpture and drawing, concerned that his students recover the sense of form, perspective, technique, but, above all, feeling in front of the elements.

The stones are everywhere. For Andrew, who works with them and distinguishes them even in the most remote places, they are a symbolic and spiritual reference. The relationship with the material base of his Work is an encounter, a recognition; and when the texture, the drumming -natural polish that time exerts in them- the emotion appears and the natural cross between matter, imagination and technique reaches stones that reach levels of otherness, they become water, air, plastic presence.

The exhibition of Andrés Vélez at the Maja Museum is the possibility of presenting the work of a young artist who aims to generate an unexplored perception of nature that surrounds everyone’s steps.

Saúl Alvarez Lara
